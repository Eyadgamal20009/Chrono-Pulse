Abstract & Introduction

Understanding the project's foundation and objectives

Abstract

Egypt generates approximately 196 million tons of waste annually, yet only 30% is recycled, presenting a significant environmental challenge. This project addresses this issue by constructing a high-precision photogate sensor system entirely from recycled electronic components.

Through rigorous testing, the prototype achieved an accuracy of 98.7% with only 1.3% error when measuring gravitational acceleration (9.679 m/s¬≤), significantly surpassing the design requirement of ¬±5% error. This demonstrates the success of the module as both a functional laboratory instrument and a practical solution to Egypt's waste management challenges.

Construction Timeline

Phase 1: Component Acquisition

Identified required materials through scientific research. Located and acquired waste devices, extracted components, and conducted functional testing on laser sensor, laser diode, and ATmega328P microcontroller.

Phase 2: Board Preparation

Positioned ATmega328P in holder, mounted essential components (10K resistor, crystal, capacitors) on PCB, established solder connections, and programmed bootloader via USBasp.

Phase 3: Circuit Assembly

Mounted laser components on breadboard for testing, developed and verified project code, uploaded final code to microcontroller, and conducted operational testing.

Phase 4: Final Integration

Constructed enclosure with precise horizontal alignment of laser components, connected all components to project board, and completed final assembly.
